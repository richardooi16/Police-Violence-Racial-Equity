Police Brutality and Racial Equity in America

A visualisation by Richard Ooi

Black-White Disparity

Although not all of the states have high Black-White Disparity, having just a few states where the ratios are very high are a concern. Another thing to note is that most if not all countries have a non-negative Black-White Disparity, which means there exist some inequality, even if it is very slight amount.

Annual Police Homicidal Cases

In these charts below, we can see the average number of murders commited by the police annually. As we can see, the numbers in the Homicide Rate for Black People have higher numbers on average compared to the numbers in the Homicide Rate for White People. Something to note is that the numbers in states such as Nevada and New Mexico have extremely high rates, reaching close to 30 average annual homicides.

Race Breakdown

Here we can see the distribution of race of the people killed by the police. Although white people have the highest percentage, they also have the highest population in the United States. Therefore, it is reasonable that the percentage being more skewed towards white people. However, other races although have a lower population still have quite a high percentage. There are also alot of unknown races but it is usually expected for them to be non-white personnel.
